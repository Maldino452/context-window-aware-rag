# Regional Guidelines - Africa - Aurelius Consulting Group

## Transportation

Flights:
- Regional flights often required (distances, limited rail)
- Book through TravelHub or reputable airlines
- Confirm flight schedules (can change frequently)
- Allow buffer time for connections

Ground:
- Taxis: Use hotel-recommended services or pre-arranged pickups
- Ride-hailing: Available in major cities (Uber, Bolt)
- Private drivers: Recommended for multi-day trips (negotiate day rate)
- Car rental: Requires pre-approval, 4WD may be necessary in some areas
- Public transport: Quality varies significantly by city

Domestic flights:
- Book early (limited capacity)
- Reconfirm 24-48 hours before departure
- Arrive at airport 2+ hours early

## Accommodation Rates

- South Africa (Cape Town, Johannesburg): $180/night
- Nigeria (Lagos): $200/night
- Kenya (Nairobi): $160/night
- Egypt (Cairo): $150/night
- Morocco (Casablanca): $140/night
- Other major cities: $150/night
- Secondary cities: $120/night

## Hotel Selection Priority

Safety and security critical:
- International chain hotels preferred
- Hotels in business districts or secure areas
- 24-hour security presence
- Reliable backup power (generator)
- Verify water quality (bottled water provided)

## Meal Allowances

- South Africa: $75/day
- Nigeria, Kenya, Morocco: $70/day
- Egypt: $65/day
- Other regions: $65/day

## Dining Considerations

- Stick to reputable restaurants (hotel, established businesses)
- Avoid street food unless locally advised as safe
- Bottled water only (verify seal intact)
- Hotel restaurants convenient and safe option
- Western chains widely available in major cities

## Business Culture

Meetings:
- Relationship-building important (allow time)
- Punctuality expectations vary by country
- Dress conservatively, professionally
- English widely spoken in business (varies by region)
- French in North/West Africa, Portuguese in Lusophone countries

Greetings:
- Handshakes common and important
- Take time for pleasantries before business
- Business cards exchanged formally
- Titles matter (use Dr., Professor, etc.)

## Health and Safety

Required preparation:
- Check vaccination requirements (yellow fever common)
- Malaria prophylaxis for many regions
- Comprehensive travel insurance mandatory
- Register with embassy/consulate
- Download TravelSafe app and check in daily

Medical:
- International clinics in major cities
- Travel with necessary medications (extra supply)
- Water: bottled only, ice cautiously
- Food: cooked thoroughly, peel fruits

Security:
- Use hotel safe for passport, valuables
- Avoid displaying expensive items
- Pre-arrange airport pickups (don't use unmarked taxis)
- Stay in well-populated, lit areas at night
- Follow local security advice strictly

## Communication

Mobile:
- Local SIM cards recommended for extended stays
- International roaming expensive
- MTN, Vodacom widely available
- WhatsApp primary communication in many countries
- Hotel WiFi generally reliable

Emergency contacts:
- Keep embassy numbers saved
- Register trip with travel security
- Share itinerary with manager
- Daily check-ins required some locations

## Currency and Payments

Varies by country:
- South Africa: Rand (ZAR) - credit cards widely accepted
- Nigeria: Naira (NGN) - carry USD, limited ATMs
- Kenya: Shilling (KES) - credit cards in major establishments
- Egypt: Pound (EGP) - carry cash for many transactions
- Morocco: Dirham (MAD) - limited credit card acceptance

General guidance:
- Carry mix of cash and cards
- ATMs at hotels/banks safer than street locations
- Notify your bank before travel
- Keep small bills for tips, taxis
- USD/EUR accepted in many locations (tourist areas)

## Visa Requirements

Most countries require visas:
- Apply well in advance (4-6 weeks minimum)
- Some offer visa-on-arrival (verify current policy)
- Letter of invitation may be required
- Company letter stating business purpose helpful
- Visa fees reimbursable

## Time Zones

Africa spans multiple time zones:
- West Africa Time (WAT): UTC+1
- Central Africa Time (CAT): UTC+2
- East Africa Time (EAT): UTC+3
- South Africa (SAST): UTC+2

Schedule meetings considering:
- Time zone differences
- Prayer times (Muslim-majority countries)
- Power outages (schedule important calls accordingly)

## Environmental Considerations

Climate varies significantly:
- North Africa: Hot, dry (light professional clothing)
- Sub-Saharan: Tropical (humid, rainy seasons)
- Southern Africa: Temperate (seasonal variations)
- Check weather for specific destination
- Pack accordingly (layers, rain gear)

## Extended Stays

For assignments over 14 days:
- Negotiate weekly/monthly accommodation rates
- Serviced apartments available in major cities
- Consider proximity to client site (traffic severe in many cities)
- Weekly laundry services reimbursable
- Local orientation recommended
